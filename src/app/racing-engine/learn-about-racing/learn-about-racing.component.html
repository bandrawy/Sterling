How We Started

Sterling Performance is owned by Michael J. D’Anniballe and was incorporated under the laws of Michigan, November 12, 1990.

Over the years Sterling Performance has built a strong relationship with Pete Hledin, founder and owner of Douglas Marine. Douglas Marine is located in Douglas, Michigan and is the manufacturer of Skater catamarans. These boats have set records and won world championships of the American Power Boat Association, Super Boat International and Union International Motonautique Class I Circuit. We proudly supply engines for installations and record setting boats for MTI, Fountain Performance Marine, Cigarette, Outer Limits, Sonic and Nortech.

Sterling Performance has become the leader and “gold standard” in High Performance Engines with numerous World Championships and the Highest Official World Record Kilo Speed – 202.9 mph!

This experience, most pronounced in professional motor sport engine building, assures high visibility and opportunity within the marketplace to continue to lead and innovate; producing the hallmark of our reputation as the finest, fastest, most reliable engines money can buy.

Our success has sparked an ever growing wave of requests for Poker Run and Performance Pleasure Boat engines, which are becoming a large staple of our engine product offerings, including our new fuel saving, yet powerful 700 hp engine package. See our Pleasure Boat Engines.

How We Started

Sterling has much more to offer than engine testing. We have personnel with experience in combustion analysis, through the use of cylinder pressure transducers, air / fuel ratio testing with Horiba instrumentation, valve train studies, cylinder head manifold flow bench work and engine frictional analysis testing.

Sterling also has the capability of performing engine crankshaft torsional vibration testing with an in house Scientific Atlantic Dynamic Signal Analyzer. We can acquire data peculiar to a particular engine combination and a purpose build harmonic dampers to best suit the engines application.
